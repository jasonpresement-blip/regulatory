Overview

This week sees significant regulatory activity focused on satellite operations and spectrum management, with both the FCC and ISED addressing GEO/NGSO coordination, space debris mitigation, and new satellite authorizations. The FCC is also advancing major consumer protection initiatives including call center onshoring requirements and reducing barriers to IP network transitions, while NTIA continues to shape spectrum policy for emerging technologies like direct-to-device satellite services and commercial drones. Canadian regulators are actively updating technical frameworks for multiple spectrum bands including 2.5 GHz and E-band frequencies.

Decision on Request Received from SpaceX Canada Corp. for Use of E-band Frequencies in Canada

ISED decides on SpaceX's request to use E-band frequencies (71-76 GHz and 81-86 GHz) for satellite operations in Canada.

Impact: This decision could enable SpaceX Starlink gateway operations in Canada and may affect other operators' access to E-band spectrum for backhaul and satellite earth station applications.
